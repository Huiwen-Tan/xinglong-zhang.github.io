About Me


After completing my Bachelor of Arts in Natural Sciences at the Univeristy of Cambridge, I spent one year working full-time on polymer synthesis in Dr. Yugen Zhang's lab at the Institute of Bioengineering and Nanotechnology (IBN) in Singapore. I am currently a third-year student doing the EPSRC Centre for Doctoral Training course in Theory and Modelling in Chemical Sciences (TMCS) at the Physical and Theorectical Chemistry Laboratory (PTCL) in the University of Oxford. During my first year of TMCS CDT programme, I obtained a Distinction in the MSc in Theoretical and Computational Chemistry.

As part of the first year programme, I have worked under Prof. Adrian Mulholand and Prof. Fredrick Manby on projector-based embedding calculations of citrate synthase enzyme. For a second project, I worked under Prof. Robert Paton on the computational studies of C-H oxidation of hydrocarbons by metheyl(trifluoromethyl)dioxirane (TFDO) using DFT calculations. I have since joined the Paton lab for a DPhil studies, working on computational calculations of organic reactions.
